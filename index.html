Brick Slip FAQs

At Cawarden Reclaim we supply brick slips cut from genuine reclaimed bricks, carrying the authentic surface, colour and character of the original brick. With over 35 years in the trade, our team can advise on brick type, colour matching, coverage and installation. Here are the questions we get asked most.

Brick slips are thin sections cut from the face of a full brick, typically 20–25mm thick. Reclaimed brick slips are cut from genuine old bricks, preserving the original surface, colour and weathered character. They can be bonded to walls, fireplaces and other surfaces to give the appearance of a full brick wall.
Brick slips are bonded to a prepared surface using a flexible tile adhesive or specialist brick slip adhesive. Once set, the joints can be pointed with mortar to complete the look. Most surfaces including plasterboard, cement board, blockwork and render are suitable substrates.
Yes, reclaimed brick slips are suitable for both interior and exterior use. For external applications, we recommend using a flexible, frost-resistant adhesive and ensuring the substrate is weatherproof and stable.
Coverage depends on the brick size and joint width, but as a guide you'll typically need around 48–52 slips per square metre for a standard brick size with a 10mm mortar joint. Contact our team with your measurements and we'll calculate the exact quantity for you.
Yes, we offer a free brick matching service. Send us photos of your existing brickwork and we'll find the closest match from our extensive stock of reclaimed bricks. We can then cut slips to suit your project.
Yes, we deliver nationwide across the UK. All deliveries are fully insured and tracked. Standard delivery is 2–3 working days with next-day options available. Contact our team for a delivery quote.
